Introduction


This short story is addressed to enthusiasts of the Bible codes. You will not find any Bible code matrices reproduced here. But this story should still be of great interest to the Bible codes researcher as well as codes enthusiasts who just dwell on the sidelines perusing the work of others. For you see, any Bible code matrix is only as good as the accurate text of the Tanach which underlies it.


Disclaimer: I have been deliberately vague and perhaps a little evasive in order to obscure certain details like dates and company names. I have done this in order to avoid litigation. It is unfortunate, but it's a sign of the times we live in. I don't want to be accused of violating someone's copyright or End User Licence Agreement. And I certainly don't want to be accused of slandering any person or company. I think you'll appreciate what I'm trying to accomplish as you get into the story.




Chapter 1

How I Got Started On This Quest



A good many years ago (I will call this year two because I'm trying to be vague) I became interested in the Bible codes, and purchased some software to do some personal investigation. The first thing I searched for, of course, was my own name. I found my name without much difficulty, and surrounding and intersecting my name were the names of my wives and children.


I was pleased with this result, and a little amazed at how easy it had been. The ease left me a little skeptical so I decided to do some further investigation. I searched for the name of one of my brothers. After some attempts at guessing the correct transliteration of his name, I obtained the desired matrix. It contained the name of my brother along with his three children and the name of the tiny country town that he lived in.


I was impressed, but also seriously dismayed because try as I might, I could not find the name of my brother's wife anywhere in the matrix. Looking back it seems silly, but at the time this really upset me. So I thought, "You know, I really don't need to know this stuff," and I set the software aside.


Years passed; the kids were now grown, and I was now on my third wife. I had been telling her about the Bible codes, and one day on a whim I got into my old paper files and pulled out the original matrix containing my name. I stared at it a while and then circled the letters containing the name of my most recent wife. "That was easy," I thought, and I decided to begin dabbling in the codes again.


So I bought some new software (notice that I'm not naming any companies), installed it, and did a search for my name. I got nothing! This disturbed me greatly. Had God removed my name from the Tanach because of my many sins? The very thought made me tremble. Was this even possible? Can the Tanach change? I know that with God all things are possible, but would He change the Tanach? These questions made my head spin, and I was very upset.


So I began to dig a little deeper. I tried searching for just my first name using the original Equidistant Letter Sequence (skip) from years ago. I found it with no trouble. Then I added the first letter of my last name, and had success again. But when I added the next letter of my last name, the search failed.


Next I tried searching for the remainder of my last name omitting the characters already found. Eureka! I had located the remainder of my name. So what was the problem? If I could find the two halves separately, why couldn't I find the whole. The answer was actually quite simple. The Tanach I was searching on, was different from the Tanach I had searched years before. This Tanach had an extra letter between the first and second letters of my last name.


Armed with this information, I located the original software that I had used years before. I still had one old computer that it would work on so I installed it, searched for my name, and found it with no problem. Of course this begs the question, "Which Tanach should I be using for my searches?"





Chapter 2

Let The Programming Begin



Which Tanach should I be using for my searches? I pondered the question. I supposed that if I were to make an intelligent decision, I would have to do a character by character comparison of the various Tanach versions that were available. I made an unfruitful search to find software that would do this on the web. There just didn't seem to be anything suitable out there. Could I create something myself? I had done computer programming professionally for most of my life, but I was retired now, and hadn't written a line of code in years.


I decided to give it a try, but first I had to have some Tanachs to compare. I bought and/or download every Tanach I could get my hands on. Then I set about to write my programs. "I could write very short simple programs to get the job done," I reasoned. After all, how hard could it be to compare two characters?


It was a little hard at first, but little by little my programming skills began to return. At first I did some things manually, inorder to keep the programs short and simple. But as my confidence grew so did the programs.


After a while, I had some preliminary results. I didn't even need a compare program to notice that some Tanachs had more characters than others. The shortest was 1,196,921 bytes in length, but others were longer.


Some of my Tanachs differed in as few as four places. Others had many, many more discrepancies. The most disturbing finding was that Tanachs purchased from the same (unnamed) company were different, and the differences were in different places!


I had to get to the bottom of all this. There cannot be more than one original authoritative Tanach. Without the authoritative text, any Bible code matrix produced would be suspect. I began to formulate a plan.



Chapter 3

The Ivan Panin Technique


I thought about how to discover the original text of the Tanach. Of course teams of people have been working on this problem for years ... and they've made lots of progress. But what could I possibly add to their work? And then I remembered Ivan Panin.


I first learned of Ivan Panin back in year one (being vague again). This was way before the internet, and computers of that day filled large rooms with raised floors. The term "personal computer" would have been an oxymoron. Back then I was still young and healthy. I had a freshly minted masters in Computer Science, a young wife, and a good job at the Teenie Tiny Machine Company (not their real name).


The problem with the Machine Company was that they owned my thoughts. In order to insure a steady flow of income I had been forced to agree that any ideas that occured to me – even those conceived on my own time – belonged to the company. I was supposed to write all these thoughts in a bound notebook, and surrender them to the company when the notebook got full. If any of my ideas became a patent, I would receive one "patent point". If I accumulated about twenty or so of these points, I could redeem them for something tangible like a toaster, a steam iron, or a pressure cooker.


But what did any of this have to do with Ivan Panin? I first heard of Panin from a tract published by a group in Chicago. The tract was titled "The Inspiration of the Scriptures Scientifically Demonstrated". (Before the internet people published their ideas on small leaflets called tracts, and gave these out to anyone who showed an interest.) The tract went on about patterns and numbers in Scripture, multiples of seven, and how you could count things to determine the authenticity of the text.


I was intrigued. I would like to put the biblical texts (and Panin's ideas) to the test by writing a computer program, submitting the texts to the program, and seeing what I could find. But there were problems with this idea. The only computers I had access to belonged to the Machine Company, and if I wrote any programs, they too became the property of the company.


How to proceed? I didn't. I bought everything that was in print by Ivan Panin. I read his works with interest, and that's as far as it went.


Fast forward to year three. I'm now old and retired. I have no wives or children to distract me, I have all the time in the world. I have a computer that would have been the envy of the Machine Company. And best of all, my thoughts belong to myself.


It was time to get busy!




Chapter 4

Panin Scores And Variant Readings


My first attempt at resolving a variant reading involved Genesis 8:17. There's one word in this verse that can be spelled two different ways. I decided to write a program to help me reslove the spelling variation. Ivan Panin had a variety of mathmatical techniques he used to determine the authenticity of a biblical text. Of course Ivan lived a hundred years ago. So he did his calculations with a pencil, and his searching with his eye balls! There were some things that he did that I just wasn't going to be able to do, but I could count consonants, vowels, and words. I could total the things that I counted, and factor the totals. I talked to the Lord about what to do with all these numbers. He gave me the concept of the dominant factor.


The dominant factor is simply the one that occurs the most often. In my Genesis 8:17 experiment I first tried evaluating the single verse. I calculated the numbers once for each spelling variant. The results were disappointing. No numbers jumped off the screen at me. It was very inconclusive.


Then the Lord gave me more inspiration. The verse needed to be evaluated in context. I decided to do the numbers on a complete pericope. A pericope is a section of the Bible that pertains to a single subject. So I worked on finding just the right range of verses that would allow me to make an intelligent decision. It wasn't hard to pick the verses. Nearly all Bibles have sections of text with subject headings. I just followed the lead of my predecessors.


This time the results were encouraging. The dominant factor was 61. The runner up was 271. I was encouraged by these results, but felt I needed more evidence. My intuition was that 61 and 271 had meanings. I knew what I should do. I got electronic copies of the Strongs Lexicon and the Brown-Driver-Briggs lexicon and computed the values of all the words. Then I sorted them in numeric order. I thought I would simply look up the number 61, and the number 271 in the lexicon to see what they mean. The results were disappointing to say the least. There were lots of words that totaled 61 or 271. The answer was not to be found in the lexicon. I had wasted a few weeks of work.


I talked to the Lord about my failure. As I sat in my bed talking to the Lord, and thinking about the problem He gave me a small vision. It was of a window moving across the text. As it moved it grew larger and smaller as necessary. I understood immediately. Just as the verse Genesis 8:17 had to be evaluated in context. The meaning of 61 and 271 had to be determined in context too. I wrote the moving window program, and discovered that 61 had a meaning which agreed with the subject of the pericope. The other number, 271, was meaningless in this context.

I was very happy! And now I was highly motivated to put all the pieces together and attack the problem head on.


It will probably take me the rest of my life to complete this project. I'm sure the Lord has lots more things to show me along the way. It's called walking by faith. I'm excited. I'm also very humbled that the Lord has allowed me to do this. The glory is His. It has pleased Him to use me, the least of all His servants.
